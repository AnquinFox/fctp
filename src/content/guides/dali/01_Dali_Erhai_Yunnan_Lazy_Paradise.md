# Dali & Erhai Lake | 3 Days of Slow Yunnan Living

> 📅 July 2026 · 📍 Yunnan, China · ⏱️ 3 Days

---

## 🌊 Why Dali

Altitude: 2,000m above sea level.
Spring-like climate year-round. Average 15°C.
Bai ethnic minority homeland. 1,300 years of history.
Erhai Lake: 250 km². One of China's largest highland lakes.
Cangshan Mountains: 19 peaks. Snow-capped from November to April.

**Best time to visit:** March to October.
March-April: cherry blossoms, wildflowers.
July-August: rainy season, bring a jacket.
September-October: clearest skies, harvest season.

**Getting there:**
Kunming → Dali: high-speed train, 2 hours, ¥145 / ~$20.
Dali Airport (DLU): direct flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu.
Airport → Old Town: airport bus, 40 min, ¥20 / ~$3.
Dali bus station is 15 km south of Old Town.

**Getting around:**
Bicycle rental: ¥30-50 / day (~$4-7).
Electric scooter: ¥60-80 / day (~$8-11).
Taxi start at ¥7. Rides within town: ¥10-20.
DiDi (Chinese Uber) works in Dali.

## Day 1 | Dali Old Town + Three Pagodas

![Erhai Lake with blue water and Cangshan Mountains](/images/dali/dali_erhai_lake_blue_water_mountains_001.webp)

### Morning — Dali Ancient Town (大理古城)

Walk the 1,200-year-old stone streets.
Founded in 738 AD by the Nanzhao Kingdom.
Walls rebuilt during Ming Dynasty, 1382.
Nine streets in a grid layout. Still intact.

**South Gate (南门):** main entrance.
Bus 2 or 4 from Dali bus station.
Free entry. Open 24 hours.

Walk Renmin Road (人民路) north to south.
Independent shops. No chain stores.
Local Bai tie-dye textiles everywhere.
Try a Bai minority shop — hand-dyed scarves ¥20-50.

**Zhonghe Street (中和路):**
Old tea houses. Sit and watch the world pass.
Pu'er tea ¥10-30 per pot.

**Foreigner Street (洋人街):**
More touristy. Cafés, bars, live music at night.
Good for people-watching. Skip the overpriced shops.

**Practical:**
Free to walk. No entrance fee.
Best time: 9-11 AM or 4-6 PM.
Avoid weekends — domestic crowds peak.
Spend 2-3 hours.

---

### Afternoon — Chongsheng Temple & Three Pagodas (崇圣寺三塔)

![Three Pagodas of Chongsheng Temple against mountain backdrop](/images/dali/dali_chongsheng_three_pagodas_temple_mountain_001.webp)

Dali's most iconic landmark.
Main pagoda: 69.13m tall. Built in 824-840 AD.
Two smaller pagodas: 42.19m each. Built 937-944 AD.
UNESCO tentative list site.

**Temple complex:**
One of the largest Buddhist temples in Yunnan.
27 halls. Covers 70,000 m².
 rebuilt 2005 after earthquake damage.

**Three Pagodas Reflection Pool:**
The money shot. Reflection in still water.
Best light: late afternoon, golden hour.
Photographers line up by 4 PM.

**Practical:**
Address: Chongsheng Temple Road, 1.5 km north of Old Town.
Bus 8 from South Gate. 15 min. ¥2.
Or cycle: 20 min ride.
**Ticket: ¥75 / ~$10.** Includes temple + pagodas.
Hours: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM (summer).
Allow 2-3 hours.
Tip: buy the combo ticket, not just the pagoda view.

---

### Evening — Foreigner Street Night Scene

Dusk is when Dali comes alive.
Street vendors set up at 6 PM.
Bai minority BBQ: skewers ¥3-5 each.
Try rushan (乳扇) — grilled cheese fan, ¥5-8.
Local beer: Dali Beer (大理啤酒), ¥5-10.

Live music from 8 PM onward.
Jazz, folk, Chinese indie.
Cover charge: ¥0-50 depending on the bar.

**Recommended bars:**
Bad Monkey — backpacker favorite, live bands.
The Church — converted chapel, cocktails.
Dali Beer House — local brews, no frills.

**Windflower Snow Moon (风花雪月):**
The four famous sights of Dali.
Wind of Shangguan, Flower of Erhua,
Snow of Cangshan, Moon of Erhai.
You'll hear this phrase everywhere.

## Day 2 | Erhai Lake Cycling + Xizhou

![Dali Old Town streets with mountains in background](/images/dali/dali_old_town_street_mountains_001.webp)

### Morning — Erhai Lake East Coast Cycle

120 km total circumference.
East coast: 40 km one way. Flat, paved.
Most scenic section: Shuanglang to Caihe.
Ride time: 3-4 hours one way.

**Start point:**
Rent bike in Old Town. Most shops on Renmin Road.
Electric scooter recommended for the full loop.
Price: ¥30 bicycle, ¥60 electric scooter per day.

**Route:**
Old Town → Caicun → Shuanglang (25 km)
Shuanglang → Caihe → Xizhou (15 km)
Total: 40 km. 3-4 hours with stops.

**Shuanglang (双廊):**
Fishing village turned artist enclave.
White houses. Blue doorways. Bougainvillea.
Annie's Café — Erhai Lake views, ¥30-50/lunch.
Good rest stop. 1-2 hours.

**Caihe (才村):**
Quieter than Shuanglang.
Bai elder women selling homemade cheese.
Photographer's favorite — less crowded.

**Practical:**
Start before 9 AM. Afternoon winds pick up.
Bring sunscreen. Altitude = strong UV.
Water and snacks available at every village.
Helmet: not provided. Bring your own or ask.

---

### Afternoon — Xizhou Old Town (喜洲古镇)

![Xizhou Old Town with traditional Bai architecture](/images/dali/dali_xizhou_old_town_bai_architecture_001.webp)

Less touristy than Dali Old Town.
Bai architecture at its finest.
Three courtyards, five courtyards, compound homes.
30,000 residents. Still a real town.

**Xizhou Morning Market:**
Best at 7-9 AM. Raw, unfiltered.
Bai women in traditional dress selling vegetables.
Rice noodles ¥5-8. Fresh tofu ¥3.
Not for tourists. That's why it's great.

**Bai Architecture:**
Traditional Bai homes have a specific layout.
Three rooms facing south, one facing east.
Intricate wood carvings on doors and windows.
Visit Yan's Old House (严家大院): ¥30 entry.

**Zhoucheng Village (周城):**
10 min bike ride from Xizhou.
Center of Bai tie-dye production.
Watch artisans at work. Buy direct.
Cheapest prices in Dali here.

**Food in Xizhou:**
Xizhou Baba (喜洲粑粑): ¥5-10.
Pan-fried flatbread. Stuffed with meat or sugar.
Get it from the old ladies on the main street.
Fresh from the pan — crispy outside, soft inside.

Rushan (乳扇): ¥3-5.
Bai cheese made from goat milk.
Rolled on a stick. Sweet or savory.
Try the fried version — ¥8.

**Practical:**
Address: Xizhou Town, 18 km north of Dali Old Town.
Bus 8 from Dali bus station. 30 min. ¥5.
Or cycle from Erhai route: direct.
Allow 2-3 hours for the town.

---

### Evening — Return to Dali Old Town

Sunset at Erhai Lake if you're still on the bike.
Golden hour light on the water is exceptional.
Temple of the Dragon Lady (龙女庙) viewpoint.

Dinner in Old Town:
Bai-style grilled fish: ¥40-60 per fish.
Local vegetables: ¥15-25 per dish.
Rice wine: ¥10-20 per bowl.

## Day 3 | Cangshan Mountain + Nanzhao Island

![Erhai Lake sunrise with golden morning light](/images/dali/dali_erhai_sunrise_morning_light_001.webp)

### Morning — Cangshan Mountain (苍山)

19 peaks. Highest: 4,122m (Malong Peak).
Three cable car lines:
1. Zhonghe Cable Car (中和索道): ¥100 round trip.
   Goes to Zhonghe Temple. 2,800m.
   Easiest. Good for first-timers.
2. Gantong Cable Car (感通索道): ¥110 round trip.
   Goes to Gantong Temple. 2,600m.
   More nature, less crowds.
3. Washburn Cable Car (洗马潭索道): ¥282 round trip.
   Goes to the top. 3,900m.
   **Best views. Worth the price.**

**Washburn Cable Car route:**
Mid-station at 3,200m. Transfer to second car.
Summit: 3,900m. Panoramic Erhai Lake below.
Temperature drops 10-15°C from base.
Bring a jacket. Seriously.

**Walking trails:**
Jade Belt Trail (玉带云游路): 12 km.
Hugs the mountainside at 2,600m.
Takes 3-4 hours. Moderate difficulty.
Start from Zhonghe, end at Washburn station.
Best trail in Cangshan.

**Practical:**
Address: Cangshan Mountain, west of Dali Old Town.
Cable car stations: 10-15 min by taxi from Old Town.
**Washburn: ¥282 / ~$39.** Opens 8:30 AM.
Allow 4-5 hours for the mountain.
Water and snacks at the cable car base. Nothing at summit.

---

### Afternoon — Boat to Nanzhao Island (南诏岛)

![Nanzhao Island temple on Erhai Lake](/images/dali/dali_nanzhao_island_lake_temple_001.webp)

Small island in Erhai Lake.
15 minutes by boat from Caihe dock.
Once a Nanzhao Kingdom royal retreat.

**Boat schedule:**
Departures every 30 minutes.
Last boat back: 5:00 PM (summer).
Ticket: ¥50 / ~$7. Includes return.

**On the island:**
Nanzhao Island: walkable in 1-2 hours.
Pavilions, gardens, old temple.
Island park with lake views.
Quiet. Most visitors stay on the main shore.

**Jinsuo Island (金梭岛):**
Alternative island. 20 min by boat from Caihe.
More authentic fishing village.
Fewer tourists. Bai minority residents.
Try fresh lake fish: ¥30-50.

**Practical:**
Boat dock: Caihe, 15 km north of Old Town.
Bus 8 + taxi, or cycle from Xizhou.
Bring sunscreen and water.
Island has one small café. Limited options.

---

### Evening — Slow Down

Last night in Dali. Don't rush.

Walk the Old Town walls at sunset.
Watch the light change over Erhai Lake.
Sky turns pink at 7:30 PM in summer.

Dinner:
Bai Hot Pot (白族火锅): ¥80-120 for two.
Local mushrooms: Yunnan specialty.
Erxiang Road (二向路) has good local spots.

Or:
Night market near South Gate.
Grilled yak meat: ¥8-10 per skewer.
Rice noodles in chicken broth: ¥12.
Hawthorn juice: ¥5.

## 🏨 Where to Stay

**Old Town (best for first-timers):**
Walking distance to everything.
Boutique hotels: ¥200-500/night (~$28-69).
Hostels: ¥50-100/night (~$7-14).
Book on Trip.com or Ctrip.

**Shuanglang (best for views):**
Lakeside rooms. Erhai Lake right outside.
More expensive: ¥300-800/night (~$42-110).
Quieter. Romantic.

**Xizhou (best for authenticity):**
Bai courtyard homestays.
¥150-300/night (~$21-42).
Real village life. Market mornings.

## 💡 Practical Tips

**Altitude:**
Dali is at 2,000m. Some visitors feel mild breathlessness.
Drink water. Take it slow on Day 1.
Cangshan summit is 3,900m. Bring warm layers.

**Money:**
Cash works everywhere. WeChat Pay dominant.
ATMs at Dali bus station and Old Town.
Foreign cards accepted at hotels and larger restaurants.

**Weather:**
Morning: 12-15°C. Afternoon: 20-25°C.
Sunny mornings, cloudy afternoons common.
Always carry a light rain jacket.
UV index high. Sunscreen mandatory.

**Language:**
Mandarin widely spoken. Bai language in villages.
English minimal. Download offline translation app.
Google Translate works offline with Chinese.

**Safety:**
Dali is very safe. Petty crime rare.
Watch your belongings at night markets.
Bicycle theft possible. Use a lock.

**Apps to download:**
DiDi (ride-hailing).
Baidu Maps (Google Maps doesn't work in China).
Trip.com / Ctrip (hotels, tickets).
WeChat (messaging, payments).
