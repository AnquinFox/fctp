## Day 1: Ancient Wonders & City Walls

---

### 🏛Scene 1: The Terracotta Army

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_terracleota_army_museum_hall_soldiers_pots.webp)

> *Nothing prepares you for the moment you walk into Pit 1. Rows upon rows of life-size soldiers staring back at you, each with a different face. I stood there for a good five minutes without moving.*

**Step 1:** Take Metro Line 9 to Huaqing Pool Station (Half). The ride from the city center takes about 40 minutes —grab a window seat, the scenery gets greener as you go.

**Step 2:** Buy tickets at the entrance (120 / ~$17). Pro tip: buy them online at least a day in advance to skip the queue entirely.

**Step 3:** Head straight to Pit 1 —it's the most jaw-dropping. Then loop through Pits 2 and 3, which are smaller but show more detail. Don't skip the museum section with the bronze chariots.

📍 **Address:** Lintong District, Xi'an 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 9 →?Huaqing Pool Station 
🕐**Hours:** 8:30 AM —5:00 PM (last entry 4 PM) 
💰 **Price:** 120 (~$17)

💡 **Pro tip:** Go on a weekday morning to avoid the tour groups. By 2 PM the crowds thin out too, but the morning light through the museum windows is better for photos.

### 🐼 Scene 2: Cycling the Ancient City Wall

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_ancient_city_wall_bicycle_sunset_brickwork.webp)

> *The Xi'an City Wall is the best-preserved city wall in all of China. I rented a bike at sunset and rode the full 13.7 kilometers. The golden light hitting the brickwork while the city below buzzed —pure magic.*

**Step 1:** Enter at the South Gate (Half), the most photogenic entrance. The gate complex itself is worth exploring.

**Step 2:** Rent a bike at the top (45 for 2 hours / ~$6). Single bikes are more fun, but tandem bikes are available if you're with someone.

**Step 3:** Ride the full loop. It takes about 1.5— hours at a leisurely pace. The east side has the best sunset views.

📍 **Address:** Zhongxin Square, Beilin District (? 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 2 →?Yongningmen Station (Half) 
🕐**Hours:** 8:00 AM —10:00 PM (extended hours in summer) 
💰 **Price:** 54 entry + 45 bike rental (~$14 total)

💡 **Pro tip:** Rent the bike from the South Gate and return it there —most rental spots close at 9 PM, but some vendors along the wall let you keep it later. Bring water; there's little shade up top.

**Step 2:** Hit these must-try stalls: roujiamo (roujiamo Xi'an's famous "Chinese hamburger"), yang rou chuan (yang rou chuan lamb skewers), and huanggui gui (, osmanthus rice wine).

**Step 3:** For dessert, try the biangbiang noodles shop on the side streets —thinner and more authentic than the tourist spots on the main drag.

📍 **Address:** Huimin Street, Lianhu District () 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 2 →?Bell Tower Station (ゼ?

## Day 2: Pagodas, Museums & Tang Dynasty Nights

---

### 🕯Scene 4: Giant Wild Goose Pagoda & Fountain Show

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_great_wild_goose_pagoda_fountain_music_show.webp)

> *The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda is iconic during the day, but at night? It transforms. The musical fountain show with colorful lights reflecting on the water is pure spectacle —and it's free.*

**Step 1:** Arrive by late afternoon to explore the pagoda and the surrounding temple complex. Climb to the top for a 360-degree view of Xi'an.

**Step 2:** Grab dinner at one of the restaurants around the North Square —the dumpling banquet restaurants here are actually quite good and not as overpriced as you'd expect.

**Step 3:** Settle in at the North Square fountain for the evening show. Shows run every hour from 8 PM to 9:30 PM in summer. Grab a spot 15 minutes early —the front rows fill up fast.

📍 **Address:** 4 Yanta South Road, Yanta District (?? 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 3 or 4 →?Dayanta Station (ч) 
🕐**Hours:** Pagoda 8 AM —6 PM; Fountain show 8 PM —9:30 PM (summer) 
💰 **Price:** Pagoda 50 (~$7); Fountain show FREE

💡 **Pro tip:** The south square fountains are less crowded than the north. If you want the best photos of the pagoda lit up at night, stand on the bridge over the reflecting pool on the north side.

### Scene 5: Mount Hua —The Plank Walk

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_huashan_mountain_cliff_plank_walk_dramatic.webp)

> *This is not for the faint of heart. The plank walk on Mount Hua is literally wooden boards bolted to a sheer cliff face, hundreds of meters above the valley. My hands were shaking. My heart was pounding. I loved every second.*

**Step 1:** Take an early morning high-speed train from Xi'an North Station to Huashan North Station (Half) —only 30 minutes. From the station, take a taxi or bus to the West Peak cable car entrance.

**Step 2:** Take the West Peak cable car up (140 round trip / ~$20), then hike to the plank walk. The trail from West Peak takes about 1.5 hours.

**Step 3:** Do the plank walk (30 / ~$4 extra for the harness). You'll be clipped to a safety wire the whole time. Go slow, take photos, breathe.

📍 **Address:** Huashan Scenic Area, Huayin City, Shaanxi (タHalf) 
🚇 **Transport:** High-speed train to Huashan North Station, then taxi (20) 
🕐**Hours:** Cable car 7 AM —7 PM (summer); Plank walk closes at 4 PM 
💰 **Price:** 160 entry + 140 cable car + 30 plank walk (~$46 total)

💡 **Pro tip:** Do the full West-to-East peak route for the best experience —cable car up West, hike through South and East peaks, cable car down North. Bring gloves for the chain sections. Start before 9 AM to avoid afternoon crowds.

### 🏺 Scene 6: Shaanxi History Museum

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_shaanxi_history_museum_artifacts_gold_silver.webp)

> *If the Terracotta Army is about scale, the Shaanxi History Museum is about finesse. Tang Dynasty gold vessels, jade burial suits, and bronze mirrors that are 2,000 years old —all displayed in elegant glass cases with perfect lighting.*

**Step 1:** Get the free ticket for the basic exhibition —just show up with your passport. For the Tang Dynasty gold and silver exhibition (the real treasure trove), buy the 30 add-on ticket.

**Step 2:** Start on the 1st floor with the prehistoric and Zhou Dynasty artifacts, then move up to the 2nd floor Tang Dynasty collection. The gold crown and silver plate are the stars.

**Step 3:** Don't skip the underground tomb replica gallery —it shows cross-sections of actual excavated tombs with murals intact.

📍 **Address:** 91 Xiaozhai North Road, Yanta District (ㄥ?1? 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 2 →?Xiaozhai Station (? 
🕐**Hours:** 9 AM —5:30 PM (closed Mondays) 
💰 **Price:** Free (basic); 30 (~$4) for gold & silver exhibition

💡 **Pro tip:** Go on a weekday morning. The museum is closed on Mondays. If you can only go on a weekend, arrive right at 9 AM when the doors open —by 11 AM it's shoulder-to-shoulder.

### 🍜 Scene 7: Biangbiang Noodles

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_biangbiang_noodles_handmade_street_food_bowl.webp)

> *You can't visit Xi'an without trying biangbiang noodles. The name is so complex that you literally can't type it on most keyboards. The noodles are thick, chewy, and drenched in chili oil —pure comfort food.*

**Step 1:** Find a local shop (not a chain). The best biangbiang noodles are in small, no-frills restaurants where the chef makes the noodles fresh in front of you.

**Step 2:** Order the classic version with garlic, chili oil, and vegetables. The noodle is about 3 fingers wide —don't be intimidated by the size.

**Step 3:** Add a side of roujiamo and a bowl of tangyuan (sweet rice balls) for the full Xi'an food experience.

📍 **Where to find it:** Every neighborhood has a good spot. Try the streets around the Bell Tower or the alleys behind Drum Tower for local favorites. 
🕐**Hours:** Most noodle shops open 11 AM —9 PM 
💰 **Price:** 15—5 per bowl (~$2—)

💡 **Pro tip:** The name "biangbiang" comes from the sound the dough makes when slapped against the counter during preparation. If you see someone making them by hand, that's the real deal —walk in.

## Day 3: Tang Dynasty Nights & Hidden Temples

### 👘 Scene 8: Tang Dynasty Paradise (Tang Paradise)

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_tang_paradise_performers_costumes_lights.webp)

> *Tang Paradise is like stepping into a Tang Dynasty painting that came alive. The performers in elaborate costumes, the colorful stage lights, the grand palace architecture —it's immersive theater meets theme park.*

**Step 1:** Arrive by 6 PM to explore the park before the evening show. The garden architecture is stunning in the golden hour light.

**Step 2:** Catch the evening performance (included with park admission). It's a large-scale outdoor show with hundreds of performers —the Tang Dynasty dance sequences are mesmerizing.

**Step 3:** After the show, walk through the illuminated streets of the Tang Town within the park. Street performers, traditional music, and the chance to dress up in Tang Dynasty costumes.

📍 **Address:** 24 Fengqing Road, Qujiang New District (No.24 Fengqing Road, Qujiang New District 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 3 →?Daming Palace West Station, then taxi 
🕐**Hours:** 9 AM —10 PM (evening shows at 8 PM) 
💰 **Price:** 120 (~$17)

💡 **Pro tip:** The costume rental shops inside are fun but overpriced. Buy a simple costume on Taobao before your trip for 30 and bring it with you —way cheaper and more authentic.

Walking through the ruins at dawn, with spring greenery sprouting among the stone foundations, you feel the weight of history.*

**Step 2:** Walk the main axis from south to north. The foundations of the Hanyuan Hall (? are the highlight —the largest palace hall in Chinese history.

**Step 3:** Visit the exhibition hall inside the Danfeng Gate for a 3D model showing what the palace looked like in its prime. The contrast between the model and the ruins outside is breathtaking.

📍 **Address:** 38 Longshou Road, Xincheng District (No.38 Longshou Road, Xincheng District 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 2 →?Daming Palace Station (ф) 
🕐**Hours:** 8:30 AM —6 PM (summer); 9 AM —5:30 PM (winter)

### 🕊Scene 10: Small Wild Goose Pagoda

![img](/images/xian/local_generated/xian_small_wild_goose_pagoda_temple_courtyard_autumn.webp)

> *The Small Wild Goose Pagoda is Xi'an's best-kept secret. No massive crowds, no noise —just an ancient Buddhist pagoda in a quiet temple courtyard with ginkgo trees. In autumn, the golden leaves framing the pagoda create one of the most peaceful scenes I've ever witnessed.*

**Step 1:** Enter the Xi'an Museum complex (which includes the pagoda). Start with the museum exhibits —the Shaanxi history collection here rivals the bigger museum but with a fraction of the crowds.

**Step 2:** Walk through the temple grounds to the pagoda itself. The ginkgo tree in the courtyard is centuries old and absolutely stunning in autumn.

**Step 3:** Sit on one of the stone benches and just absorb the atmosphere. Monks walk through the grounds, incense drifts through the air, and the world feels a million miles away.

📍 **Address:** 66 Youyi West Road, Beilin District (No.66 Youyi West Road, Beilin District 
🚇 **Metro:** Line 2 →?Nanshaomen Station (Half◢ㄧ) 
🕐**Hours:** 9 AM —6 PM (closed Mondays) 
💰 **Price:** Free (museum); 50 (~$7) for pagoda interior

💡 **Pro tip:** Visit in late October or early November when the ginkgo leaves turn gold. The contrast of golden leaves against the ancient pagoda is photographer's heaven. Arrive at opening for the best light.

## 🗺Practical Tips

### Getting Around
- **Metro:** Xi'an's metro is clean, cheap, and covers all major attractions. Get a transit card at any station (20 deposit + 50 credit).
- **Bike sharing:** HelloBike and Meituan bikes are everywhere —great for short trips.
- **Taxi:** Use DiDi (Chinese Uber) for convenience. Most rides within the city are 15—0.

### Food Budget
- **Street food meals:** 30—0 (~$4—)
- **Restaurant meals:** 80—50 (~$11—1)
- **Fine dining:** 200—00 (~$28—6)

### Weather
- **July:** Hot and humid, 25—5C (77—5F). Carry water and sunscreen.
- **Best months:** March—ay and September—ovember for comfortable temperatures.

### Money
- China is largely cashless. WeChat Pay and Alipay are accepted everywhere. International visitors can now link foreign credit cards to these apps.
