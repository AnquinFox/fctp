# 🏯 Classic Golden Triangle

![Classic Golden Triangle - Great Wall](/images/beijing/greatwall_aerial_panorama_gw.webp)
> : Beijing → Xi'an → Shanghai

> 📅 July 2026 | 📍 First-time visitors to China | ⏱️ 7 Days / 6 Nights

If you've never been to China and you've only got a week, this is the route. Beijing for the history, Xi'an for the soul, Shanghai for the sparkle. I've done this exact trip twice — once solo, once with my parents — and it works every single time.

The high-speed rail connects all three cities, so you don't need to deal with airports after arrival. Total transport cost for the whole triangle? Roughly ¥1,200 / ~$165. That's cheaper than a round-trip flight in most countries.

---

## 🎯 Before You Go

### Must-Have Apps
- **Alipay / WeChat Pay** — Cash is nearly dead in China. Set this up before you land (see Payment Setup Guide)
- **Didi** — China's Uber. Works in all three cities
- **12306** — Official train booking app. Book high-speed rail tickets here
- **Google Maps (with VPN)** — or use Amap (高德地图) which works without VPN
- **Pleco** — Best Chinese dictionary app. Camera translation is a lifesaver

### Best Season to Visit
- **Spring (April–May)** — Pleasant weather, blooming gardens in Suzhou/Shanghai
- **Autumn (September–October)** — Golden Beijing, comfortable temperatures
- **Avoid**: Chinese New Year (January/February), National Day Golden Week (October 1–7) — everything is packed and prices triple

## 📅 Itinerary

---

### Day 1: Beijing — Arrival & The Heart of the Empire

![Tiananmen Square & Forbidden City](/images/beijing/gugong_panorama_gw.webp)


**Theme:** Settle in, get oriented, eat everything

#### Tiananmen Square & The Forbidden City

Start at Tiananmen Square. Yes, it's huge — the largest public square in the world. But honestly, it's more impressive from photos than standing in the middle of it. Walk through to the Forbidden City, and *that's* where the magic happens.

The Forbidden City is enormous. You could spend 4 hours in there and still miss things. My advice: enter from the south (Meridian Gate), walk the central axis to the north, then double back to the side palaces. Don't try to see everything — pick the Hall of Supreme Harmony, the Imperial Garden, and the Clock Gallery.

- 📍 Address: 4 Jingshan Front Street, Dongcheng District
- 🚇 Metro Line 1, Tiananmen East Station, Exit A
- ⏰ Opening hours: 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Mondays)
- 💰 Entry: ¥60 / ~$8 (April–October), ¥40 / ~$5.50 (November–March)
- ⚠️ **Book online 7 days in advance** — daily visitor cap is strict

#### Jingshan Park

After the Forbidden City, walk north into Jingshan Park. Climb to the top of the hill (15 minutes) for the best panoramic view of the Forbidden City's golden rooftops. Sunset from here is unforgettable.

- 💰 Entry: ¥2 / ~$0.30

#### Wangfujing Street

Evening stroll down Beijing's most famous shopping street. The snack street (Wangfujing Snack Street) is touristy but fun — try scorpions on sticks if you dare. I didn't. My friend did. He survived.

🛏️ **Stay near**: Dongcheng District — walking distance to most Day 1 & 2 attractions

### Day 2: Beijing — Great Wall & Local Life

![Xi'an City Wall](/images/xian/xian_city_wall_003.webp)


**Theme:** The Wall, then the real Beijing

#### Mutianyu Great Wall

Skip Badaling — it's the most famous section but also the most crowded. Mutianyu is just as spectacular, less packed, and has a cable car + toboggan ride down. Yes, a toboggan. On the Great Wall. It's as awesome as it sounds.

Take the bus from Dongzhimen (about 1.5 hours) or book a private car (¥200–300 / ~$28–42).

- 📍 Address: Mutianyu Village, Huairou District
- ⏰ Opening hours: 7:30 AM – 5:00 PM
- 💰 Entry: ¥40 / ~$5.50 + cable car ¥120 / ~$17 round trip
- 🕐 Spend 3–4 hours hiking the wall

**Pro tip:** Hike from Tower 14 to Tower 20 for the quieter, more scenic stretch. Bring water — there's nothing on the wall.

#### Nanluoguxiang Hutong

Back in the city, wander through this 800-year-old alleyway. It's been "renovated" into a hipster lane with cafés and boutiques, but the hutong architecture is still beautiful. Duck into the side alleys for the real stuff — old men playing chess, laundry hanging between buildings, cats everywhere.

- 🚇 Metro Line 6/8, Nanluoguxiang Station
- 💰 Free to walk around

#### Peking Duck Dinner

You can't leave Beijing without eating Peking duck. I recommend **Da Dong** (大董) — the skin is impossibly crispy, the meat juicy, and the pancakes thin as paper. Budget ¥150–200 / ~$21–28 per person.

Alternative: **Quanjude** (全聚德) — more famous, slightly more touristy, still good.

### Day 3: Beijing — Temple of Heaven & Travel to Xi'an

![Terracotta Warriors](/images/xian/xian_terracotta_warriors_001.webp)


**Theme:** Morning culture, afternoon bullet train

#### Temple of Heaven

Wake up early and head to the Temple of Heaven park. This is where emperors prayed for good harvests. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests is one of China's most iconic buildings — triple-gabled, perfectly round, no nails in the entire structure.

But the real highlight is the park itself. At 6 AM, you'll find locals doing tai chi, playing erhu, singing opera, and playing cards. It's Beijing at its most authentic.

- 📍 Address: 1 Tiantan East Road, Dongcheng District
- 🚇 Metro Line 5, Tiantan Dongmen Station
- ⏰ 6:00 AM – 9:00 PM
- 💰 ¥15 / ~$2 (park), ¥34 / ~$4.70 (Hall of Prayer)

#### Afternoon: Beijing → Xi'an by High-Speed Train

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Route | Beijing West → Xi'an North |
| Duration | ~4.5 hours |
| Price | ¥520 / ~$72 (2nd class) |
| Frequency | Every 30 minutes |
| Booking | 12306 app or trip.com |

Pro tip: Book a window seat on the left side. You'll see the landscape shift from flat farmland to loess plateaus to mountains. It's like watching a nature documentary in real time.

Arrive in Xi'an evening. Check in near the Bell Tower — it's the most central location.

### Day 4: Xi'an — Terracotta Warriors & City Walls

![Shanghai skyline](/images/shanghai/shanghai_bund_night_002.webp)


**Theme:** Ancient empire, living history

#### Terracotta Warriors (兵马桶)

This is one of those places that actually lives up to the hype. 8,000 life-sized soldiers, each with a unique face, buried for 2,200 years to guard Emperor Qin Shi Huang in the afterlife. The scale is staggering.

Take the tourist bus 5 (游5) from Xi'an train station (306 Bus), about 1 hour. Or book a Didi for ¥120–150 / ~$17–21.

- 📍 Address: Lintong District, ~40km east of Xi'an
- ⏰ 8:30 AM – 6:00 PM (March–November)
- 💰 ¥120 / ~$17
- 🕐 Spend 2.5–3 hours

**My advice:** Hire a guide at the entrance (¥150–200 / ~$21–28 for a group). The site is fascinating but the stories behind each pit are what make it unforgettable. Without context, it's just a lot of statues.

#### Xi'an City Wall

The most complete ancient city wall in China. You can rent a bike and ride the full 13.7km circuit — it takes about 2 hours and the views of the city from up there are gorgeous.

- 📍 Address: Zhongxin Station area (multiple entrances)
- ⏰ 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM (south gate open latest)
- 💰 ¥54 / ~$7.50, bike rental ¥45–90 / ~$6–12

**Evening:** Walk to the Muslim Quarter (回民街) for dinner. This is one of the best street food areas in all of China. The lamb skewers (¥5–10 each), roujiamo (Chinese "hamburger," ¥8–12), and pomegranate juice (¥10) are all incredible. Come hungry.

### Day 5: Xi'an — Deep Cuts & Travel to Shanghai

![The Bund & Oriental Pearl Tower](/images/shanghai/shanghai_oriental_pearl_tower_001.webp)


**Theme:** Hidden gems, then bullet train to the future

#### Shaanxi History Museum

One of the best museums in China, and it's free. The Tang Dynasty collection is extraordinary — gold, silver, jade, and the famous Tang sancai (three-color glazed pottery). Arrive at opening (9 AM) to avoid the worst crowds.

- 📍 Address: 91 Xiaozhai East Road, Yanta District
- 🚇 Metro Line 2, Xiaozhai Station
- ⏰ 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM (closed Mondays)
- 💰 Free (passport required)

#### Big Wild Goose Pagoda

Walk from the museum to the Big Wild Goose Pagoda (about 20 minutes). Built in 652 AD to store Buddhist scriptures brought from India. The surrounding plaza is lovely in the evening with the fountain show.

- 💰 ¥50 / ~$7 (interior), free to admire from outside

#### Afternoon: Xi'an → Shanghai by High-Speed Train

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Route | Xi'an North → Shanghai Hongqiao |
| Duration | ~6 hours |
| Price | ¥900 / ~$124 (2nd class) |
| Departure | Best to leave by 1 PM |

This is the longest leg. Download some podcasts or books on your phone. The scenery is spectacular — you'll cross the Qinling Mountains and then flatten out into the Yangtze Delta.

Arrive in Shanghai evening. Check in near Nanjing Road or The Bund for the best location.

---

### Day 6: Shanghai — The Bund, French Concession & Rooftop Views

**Theme:** Colonial history meets modern glamour

#### The Bund (外滩)

Walk along the Bund at sunrise. Seriously. I know it's "a thing" to see it at night, but morning is when you have it almost to yourself. The colonial buildings on one side, the futuristic skyline of Lujiazui on the other — it's the most dramatic "old meets new" view in any city I've visited.

- 📍 Address: Zhongshan East 1st Road, Huangpu District
- 🚇 Metro Line 2/10, Nanjing East Road Station
- 💰 Free

#### Lujiazui Skyline

Cross the river (or take Metro Line 2 one stop to Lujiazui). If you're into skyscrapers, go up the Shanghai Tower (¥180 / ~$25 for the observation deck at 632m — the world's second-tallest building). I skipped it because ¥180 is steep for a view, but my friends said it's worth it on a clear day.

Instead, I walked through the Lujiazui Financial District and took photos from ground level. The Oriental Pearl Tower, Jin Mao Tower, and Shanghai World Financial Center all loom above you. It feels like standing in a sci-fi movie.

#### French Concession (法租界)

This is my favorite neighborhood in Shanghai. Tree-lined streets, Art Deco buildings, independent cafés, boutique shops. Walk along Wukang Road (武康路) — the Wukang Mansion (武康大楼) is one of the most photographed buildings in Shanghai.

**Lunch recommendation:** Find a small restaurant for xiaolongbao (soup dumplings). I like **Jia Jia Tang Bao** (佳家汤包) — ¥30–50 / ~$4–7 for a full meal.

#### Evening: Rooftop Drinks

Shanghai's rooftop bar scene is world-class. **Flair** at the Ritz-Carlton has the highest outdoor terrace in the world. **Bar Rouge** on the Bund has the best skyline view. Even if you don't drink, going up for the view is worth it.

---

### Day 7: Shanghai — Markets, Gardens & Departure

**Theme:** Last morning, last bites

#### Yuyuan Garden (豫园)

A classical Chinese garden from the Ming Dynasty. Small but exquisite — every rock, every pond, every pavilion is carefully placed. The surrounding bazaar is touristy but the garden itself is peaceful.

- 📍 Address: 218 Anren Street, Huangpu District
- ⏰ 9:00 AM – 4:30 PM
- 💰 ¥40 / ~$5.50

#### Tianzifang (田子坊)

Art galleries, craft shops, and cafés squeezed into narrow alleyways. It's like a maze — you'll get lost, and that's the point. Great for souvenirs.

#### Lunch: Shanghai's Best Noodles

End your trip with a bowl of scallion oil noodles (葱油拌面). Simple, cheap, absolutely delicious. Most noodle shops in the French Concession area do it well. ¥15–25 / ~$2–3.50.

#### Departure

From Shanghai Pudong Airport, you can fly almost anywhere in the world. If you're heading to Hong Kong, the high-speed rail takes 8 hours (or fly in 2.5 hours).

## 💡 Practical Tips

1. **Book train tickets early** — High-speed rail sells out, especially during holidays. Book 3–5 days ahead on 12306.
2. **VPN is essential** — Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube all need VPN. Download before arrival.
3. **Cash is optional but useful** — Most places accept Alipay/WeChat Pay. Keep ¥200 cash for small vendors.
4. **Temple etiquette** — Don't point at Buddha statues, don't take photos where "No Photography" signs are posted, remove hats in temples.
5. **Food safety** — Street food is generally safe in major cities. Stick to busy stalls with high turnover.
6. **Air quality** — Beijing can have bad air days. Check AQI apps and bring a mask (N95) just in case.
7. **High-speed rail is incredible** — Smooth, on-time, comfortable, and scenic. It'll ruin all other trains for you.
