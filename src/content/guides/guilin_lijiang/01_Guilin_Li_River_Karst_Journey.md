# 🚢💚 Li River: Cruising Through China's Most Beautiful Karst Landscape

> 📅 Single-Attraction Guide · 📍 Guilin–Yangshuo, Guangxi · ⏱️ 1–2 Days

---

Okay so here's the thing — I've been to a LOT of scenic rivers in my life. The Danube? Beautiful. The Mekong? Stunning. The Li River? **It literally broke my brain.** 😵

There's a reason locals say "Guilin's scenery is the best under heaven" (桂林山水甲天下), and after spending two days along the Li River, I can confirm this is not just propaganda. This river, winding 83 kilometers through some of the most dramatic karst mountains on Earth, is the kind of place that makes you question whether you're looking at a real landscape or a Chinese ink painting come to life. 🎨

The iconic misty peaks, emerald water, bamboo rafts gliding through morning fog — it's the China you've always imagined. And yes, it's the landscape on the back of the ¥20 banknote. More on that later. 💸

---

## 🌄 What Is the Li River (漓江)?

The Li River stretches from Guilin city all the way down to Yangshuo, cutting through a valley packed with over 20,000 karst peaks. These aren't your average limestone hills — they're dramatic, jagged, almost alien-looking towers of rock that rise straight up from flat rice paddies and emerald water. 🌿

The river itself is calm, clear, and impossibly green. UNESCO has recognized the Li River as a World Heritage Site, and National Geographic once ranked it as one of the world's top 10 waterways. This isn't hype — it's genuinely jaw-dropping.

## 🚢 The Guilin–Yangshuo Cruise: The Main Event

This is the absolute MUST-DO experience. The 4-hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo takes you through the most scenic stretch of the Li River, passing through some truly mind-blowing karst formations. 🏔️

### The Basics

- ⏰ **Duration**: 4–5 hours (one-way, Guilin → Yangshuo)
- 💰 **Price**: ¥320–450 (~$45–63) depending on the boat class
- 🕘 **Boarding**: Daily from 9:00–14:00
- 📍 **Departure**: Mopanshan Pier (磨盘山码头), about 30 minutes from Guilin city center
- 🎫 **Tip**: Book online at least 1 day in advance, especially during holidays!

### My Honest Experience

I boarded the cruise boat at around 9:30 AM and honestly? The first hour was just... green mountains. Which sounds amazing (and it is), but I was starting to think "is this it?" 🤔

**Then hour two hit.**

The karst peaks got taller, the river narrowed, and suddenly we were gliding through a corridor of these impossibly steep limestone towers with mist clinging to their sides. A local fisherman on a bamboo raft drifted past with cormorants perched on his boat. I literally forgot to breathe for a second. 😶

The cruise passes through several highlight spots:

**🗺️ Cruise Route Highlights:**

| Spot | What to See | When |
|------|------------|------|
| **Huangbuo Reflection** (黄布倒影) | The ¥20 banknote view! | ~2 hrs in |
| **Nine Horses Fresco Mountain** (九马画山) | 9 horses hidden in the rock face | ~2.5 hrs in |
| **Mural Hill** (画山) | Natural "paintings" on cliff walls | ~2.5 hrs in |
| **Xingping Old Town** (兴坪古镇) | Where the cruise ends — the ¥20 viewpoint! | End of cruise |

## 🏛️ Xingping Ancient Town: The Star of the Show

If there's ONE stop you absolutely cannot miss, it's **Xingping (兴坪)**. This tiny riverside village is where the cruise terminates, and it's where the most famous Li River view in China lives — the one printed on the back of the ¥20 RMB banknote. 💵

![Xingping Old Town along the Li River](/images/guilin_lijiang/lijiang_xingping_old_town_001.webp)
*Xingping Old Town — the real-life version of the ¥20 bill view* 📸

### Why Xingping Is Special

Walking through Xingping feels like stepping back in time. The cobblestone streets are lined with ancient Qing Dynasty buildings, small tea houses, and tiny noodle shops. It's touristy, yes, but in a charming way — not in a "fake ancient town selling the same souvenirs" way. 🍜

The real magic happens at the **Xingping Wharf (兴坪码头)**. Stand there, look across the river, and you'll see the exact same karst peaks that are printed on the ¥20 banknote. Same angle, same mountains, same everything. It's surreal.

### Xingping Tips

- 🕐 **Time needed**: 2–3 hours (more if you're a photographer)
- 💰 **Free** to enter the town itself
- 📸 **Best photo spot**: Xingping Wharf, facing the karst peaks across the river
- 🌅 **Sunset tip**: Head to **Old Di Village (老底村)** — a 15-minute walk — for the best sunset view over the river
- 🍜 **Eat**: Try the local beer fish (啤酒鱼) at one of the riverside restaurants

## 🎋 Yulong River Bamboo Rafting: The Quieter Alternative

Okay, if the Li River cruise feels too "big tour group" for you, let me introduce you to the **Yulong River (遇龙河)** — the Li River's quieter, more intimate little sibling. 🎋

The Yulong River runs through a narrower valley with even more dramatic karst scenery, and the best way to experience it is by bamboo raft. Think gliding through emerald water, bamboo poles dipping into the river, rice paddies and karst peaks on every side, and absolutely zero crowds. Just you, the raftman, and nature. 🌿

![Bamboo rafting on the Yulong River](/images/guilin_lijiang/lijiang_bamboo_raft_yulong_river_001.webp)
*Yulong River bamboo rafting — the most peaceful 2 hours of my trip* 🧘

### Bamboo Rafting Details

- ⏰ **Duration**: 1.5–2 hours per section
- 💰 **Price**: ¥160–320 (~$22–45) depending on the section
- 📍 **Location**: Yangshuo County, about 20 minutes from Yangshuo town center
- 🕐 **Best time**: Early morning (misty and magical) or late afternoon (golden light)
- ⚠️ **Note**: Not available during flood season (June–August, heavy rain)

### My Take

I did the Yulong River on Day 2 after the Li River cruise on Day 1. The Li River cruise was epic and dramatic. The Yulong River was... peaceful and poetic. Different vibes, both incredible. If you have time for both, absolutely do both. If you can only pick one? The Li River cruise for the "wow factor," the Yulong River for the "soul restoration." 🧘‍♂️

## 📸 Xianggong Hill: The Ultimate Photo Spot

For photography lovers, there's one place that's basically mandatory: **Xianggong Hill (相公山)**. This is THE spot for panoramic shots of the Li River bending through a sea of karst peaks. 📷

![Xianggong Hill sunset view](/images/guilin_lijiang/lijiang_sunset_hill_xianggong_001.webp)
*The view from Xianggong Hill — this is the photo that wins Instagram* 🏆

### Xianggong Hill Details

- 📍 **Location**: Xingping Town area, about 40 minutes drive from Yangshuo
- 💰 **Entrance**: ¥60 (~$8)
- ⏱️ **Time needed**: 1–2 hours (including the hike up)
- 🥾 **Hike**: About 15–20 minutes to the top (moderate difficulty, lots of stairs)
- 🌅 **Best time**: Sunrise (arrive by 5:30 AM) or sunset (golden hour)
- 📸 **Pro tip**: Bring a tripod. The long-exposure shots of mist over the river at dawn are unreal.

### Why It's Worth the Early Wake-Up

I dragged myself out of bed at 4:45 AM for the sunrise shoot and almost didn't go. But standing at the top of Xianggong Hill, watching the mist slowly lift to reveal the Li River winding through those karst peaks below... that's a moment I'll never forget. It's one of those "I can't believe this is real" experiences. 🌅

## 🕳️ Reed Flute Cave: The Underground Wonder

If you've had your fill of river and mountain views (is that even possible?), head to **Reed Flute Cave (芦笛岩)** — a 180-million-year-old limestone cave that's like stepping into another world. 🌌

![Reed Flute Cave interior](/images/guilin_lijiang/lijiang_cormorant_fishing_reed_flute_cave_001.webp)
*Reed Flute Cave — nature's cathedral of stalactites and multicolored lights* ✨

### The Cave Experience

The cave stretches about 500 meters and is filled with incredible stalactite and stalagmite formations — all lit up in dramatic multicolored lights. Yes, it's a bit touristy. Yes, the lighting is a little "disco." But honestly? It's spectacular. The formations have names like "Crystal Palace" and "Pine Tree in the Snow," and you'll spend the whole walk with your mouth hanging open. 🤩

### Reed Flute Cave Details

- 📍 **Location**: Guangming Hill (光明山), 5 km northwest of Guilin city center
- 💰 **Entrance**: ¥120 (~$17)
- ⏰ **Opening hours**: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM
- ⏱️ **Time needed**: 1–1.5 hours
- 🚇 **Getting there**: Bus 3 from Guilin city center, or taxi (~¥20)

## 🎣 Cormorant Fishing: A Dying Tradition

One of the most iconic images of the Li River is the **cormorant fisherman** — a local fisherman standing on a bamboo raft with trained cormorants, using a lantern to attract fish at night. 🐦

This tradition has been practiced on the Li River for over 1,000 years. The cormorants dive into the water, catch fish, and bring them back to the fisherman — who rewards them with small fish as treats. It's an incredible display of the bond between humans and animals.

**Where to see it**: You might spot cormorant fishermen during the Li River cruise, especially in the evening near Xingping. Some local fishermen near Yangshuo also perform demonstrations for tourists.

**Fair warning**: This tradition is fading. Many of the "cormorant fishing shows" you see are staged for tourists, and the real practitioners are getting fewer each year. If you get a chance to see the genuine article, treasure it. 🙏

## 🗓️ Best Time to Visit

Timing matters A LOT for the Li River. Here's my honest breakdown:

| Season | Verdict | Why |
|--------|---------|-----|
| **Sep–Nov** 🍂 | ⭐ **BEST** | Clearest water, less rain, golden light, fewer crowds |
| **Apr–Jun** 🌸 | ⭐ **GREAT** | Greenest scenery, water buffalo in rice paddies, warm but not hot |
| **Dec–Feb** ❄️ | ✅ Good | Fewer tourists, cool temps, sometimes misty (romantic!) |
| **Jul–Aug** 🌧️ | ⚠️ Risky | Hot + rainy = flooded river, cruise may be suspended |

**My pick**: Late September. The water is crystal clear, the rice paddies are golden, and the crowds have thinned out after the summer rush. Perfection. 🎯

## ❓ FAQ

### 1. How many days do I need for the Li River?

**Minimum 1 day** (the cruise alone is a full day). **Ideal: 2 days** — Day 1 for the Li River cruise + Xingping, Day 2 for Yulong River bamboo rafting + Xianggong Hill. If you add Reed Flute Cave, that's a half-day trip back in Guilin.

### 2. Is the Li River cruise worth the money?

**100% yes.** It's not cheap, but this is the kind of experience you'll remember for the rest of your life. The karst scenery from the water is completely different from anything you'll see on land. If budget is tight, at least do a short section by bamboo raft instead.

### 3. Can I swim in the Li River?

**No.** The Li River is not open for swimming. The water is used for drinking water supply, and boat traffic makes it unsafe. Stick to the boats and bamboo rafts!

### 4. Is Yangshuo worth staying in?

**Absolutely.** Yangshuo is a charming small town with great nightlife, cycling routes, and easy access to all the Li River attractions. Most people base themselves here after the cruise. The West Street (西街) area has tons of restaurants, bars, and cafés.

### 5. What's the best food to try in Guilin/Yangshuo?

**Must-try dishes:**
- 🍺 **Beer Fish (啤酒鱼)** — Yangshuo's signature dish, fish cooked in local beer
- 🍜 **Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉)** — iconic breakfast, available everywhere
- 🌶️ **Stuffed Li River Snails (田螺酿)** — local specialty, adventurous but worth it
- 🍖 **Braised Pork Rice (扣肉饭)** — comfort food, perfectly fatty

### 6. Is it safe to travel solo on the Li River?

**Yes, very safe.** The Li River area is one of China's most popular tourist destinations, and safety is excellent. Solo travelers, couples, families — everyone is welcome. Just book your cruise in advance during peak seasons (National Day Oct 1–7, Chinese New Year) as it sells out fast.
