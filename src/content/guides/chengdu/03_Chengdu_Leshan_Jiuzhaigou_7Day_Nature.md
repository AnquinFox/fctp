# 🐼 Sichuan Nature Explorer

![Jiuzhaigou Valley](/images/jiuzhaigou/jiuzhaigou_colorful_lake_turquoise_water_forest_001.webp)
> : Chengdu → Leshan → Jiuzhaigou

> 📅 July 2026 | 📍 Nature lovers & adventurous travelers | ⏱️ 7 Days / 6 Nights

Everyone goes to Chengdu for the pandas. Fair enough — they're adorable. But if you stop there, you're missing the best part of Sichuan. The real magic is 3 hours south at the Leshan Giant Buddha, and 8 hours north in the otherworldly valleys of Jiuzhaigou.

I grew up hearing about Jiuzhaigou from my grandparents — they called it "a place that doesn't belong to this world." After going there myself, I get it. The water is so blue it looks photoshopped. It isn't.

### Best Season to Visit
- **Autumn (October–November)** — Jiuzhaigou's peak season. The fall foliage reflected in the blue water is breathtaking
- **Spring (April–May)** — Waterfalls at full flow, fewer crowds
- **Summer (June–August)** — Hot in Chengdu (35°C+), but Jiuzhaigou is cool and pleasant
- **Avoid**: December–March — Jiuzhaigou gets cold and some trails close

## 📅 Itinerary

---

### Day 1: Chengdu — Pandas & Hotpot

![Chengdu city life](/images/chengdu/chengdu_people_park_tea_teahouse_002.webp)


**Theme:** Arrive, eat, repeat

#### Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding

Get there at 8:00 AM opening. By 10 AM, the pandas go inside to nap and you've missed the best action. Early morning is when they're active — climbing trees, eating bamboo, tumbling over each other. The baby pandas (if any are there) will make you question every life decision that didn't involve pandas.

- 📍 Address: 1375 Xiongmao Avenue, Chenghua District
- 🚇 Metro Line 3 to Panda Avenue Station + bus or Didi
- ⏰ 7:30 AM – 6:00 PM
- 💰 ¥55 / ~$7.60
- 🕐 Spend 2–3 hours

**My advice:** The main path splits left and right. Go RIGHT first — the adult panda enclosures are less crowded. Come back to the left side for the nursery and sub-adult areas.

#### Jinli Ancient Street (锦里)

Afternoon wander through this restored Qing Dynasty street. It's touristy but photogenic — red lanterns, traditional architecture, tea houses. Grab a bowl of dan dan noodles (担担面) from a street vendor for ¥12.

- 💰 Free to walk around

#### Hotpot Dinner

Chengdu hotpot is an experience. Sichuan peppercorns, dried chilies, beef tallow — it's numbing, spicy, and absolutely incredible. **Xiaolongkan** (小龙坎) or **Shu Daxia** (蜀大侠) are both reliable chains.

Budget ¥80–120 / ~$11–17 per person. Order the duck blood, beef triptych, and lotus root. Dip everything in sesame oil to cool down your mouth.

🛏️ **Stay near**: Chunxi Road area — central, good metro access

---

### Day 2: Chengdu — Tea Houses & Local Life

**Theme:** Slow living, Sichuan style

#### People's Park (人民公园)

This is where old Chengdu lives. Tea houses line the lotus pond, retirees play mahjong, couples dance under the trees. Sit down at **Heming Tea House** (鹤鸣茶社), order a bowl of jasmine tea (¥15–30), and just... exist. I spent 3 hours here on my first visit and didn't regret a minute.

- 🚇 Metro Line 2, People's Park Station
- 💰 Free (tea ¥15–30)

#### Kuanzhai Alley (宽窄巷子)

Three parallel alleys — Wide, Narrow, and Well-narrow. It's been renovated into a tourist zone, but the architecture is authentic Qing Dynasty. Good for photos, snacks, and people-watching.

#### Sichuan Opera (Chuanju)

If you can get tickets, see a Sichuan Opera performance. The face-changing act (变脸) is mind-blowing — performers change masks in the blink of an eye, and nobody can figure out how. **Shufeng Yayun** (蜀风雅韵) theater, ¥150–280 / ~$21–39.

#### Evening: Chengdu Craft Beer Scene

Chengdu has a surprisingly good craft beer scene. **The Beer Lady** (啤酒阿姨) on Yulin Road has 500+ beers from around the world. Perfect after a spicy hotpot night.

### Day 3: Chengdu → Leshan Giant Buddha

**Theme:** The world's largest stone Buddha

#### Morning: Train to Leshan

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Route | Chengdu South → Leshan |
| Duration | ~1 hour (high-speed rail) |
| Price | ¥55–75 / ~$7.60–10.40 |
| Frequency | Every 20–30 minutes |

#### Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)

Carved into a cliff face in 803 AD, this 71-meter Buddha is the largest stone Buddha in the world. Standing at its feet and looking up is genuinely awe-inspiring. The engineering required to carve this in the 8th century — without modern tools — is mind-boggling.

There are two ways to experience it:
1. **Walk down the cliff path** — Gets you up close but involves 399 steep steps (and you have to climb them all back up)
2. **Take the boat** — ¥70 / ~$10 round trip. Gives you the full frontal view from the river. Less tiring but you miss the detail

I did both. If you only pick one, do the boat — the full-face view is more photogenic.

- 📍 Address: 2435 Lingyun Road, Shizhong District, Leshan
- ⏰ 7:30 AM – 6:30 PM (varies by season)
- 💰 ¥80 / ~$11

#### Lingyun Temple

At the top of the cliff path, there's a temple complex. Less crowded than the Buddha itself, with great views of the Minjiang and Dadu rivers converging.

#### Evening: Return to Chengdu

Train back takes 1 hour. Dinner in Chengdu — try **mapo tofu** (麻婆豆腐) at Chen Mapo Tofu (陈麻婆豆腐), the original restaurant. ¥15–25 / ~$2–3.50.

### Day 4: Chengdu → Jiuzhaigou

![Leshan Grand Buddha](/images/chengdu/chengdu_leshan_buddha_river_cliff_009.webp)


**Theme:** Into the mountains

#### Getting to Jiuzhaigou

Two options:
1. **Fly to Jiuhuang Airport** (九黄机场) — 45 minutes from Chengdu, ¥800–1500 / ~$110–207 one way. Then 1.5-hour bus to park entrance
2. **Bus from Chengdu** — 8–10 hours, ¥150–200 / ~$21–28. Scenic but exhausting

I flew. Life's short. The flight itself is spectacular — you fly right over the mountains.

⚠️ **Altitude warning:** Jiuzhaigou is at 2,000–3,100m elevation. If you're coming from sea level, you might feel mild altitude symptoms (headache, shortness of breath). Stay hydrated, take it easy on Day 4.

#### Check in at Jiuzhaigou Town

The town outside the park (漳扎镇) has dozens of hotels and guesthouses. Stay here — it's the only practical option. Book in advance during peak season (October).

#### Evening: Rest & Prepare

You'll need an early start tomorrow. Buy snacks and water tonight — prices inside the park are double.

🛏️ **Stay near**: Zhangzha Town (漳扎镇), near the park entrance

---

### Day 5: Jiuzhaigou — The Blue Dream (Part 1)

![Mount Emei](/images/chengdu/chengdu_emei_mountain_temple_clouds_010.webp)


**Theme:** The valleys and lakes

#### The Park

Jiuzhaigou is shaped like a Y. The main valleys are:
- **Zechariah Valley** (则查洼沟) — longest, has the famous Long Lake and Five Flower Lake
- **Rize Valley** (日则沟) — most scenic, waterfalls and mirror-like lakes
- **Shuzheng Valley** (树正沟) — entrance valley, waterfalls and village scenery

Start with **Rize Valley** (right branch). Take the park bus to the top (Nuorilang Station) and work your way down. This is the classic route and gives you the best photos.

#### Must-See Stops

**Five Flower Lake (五花湖)** — The star of Jiuzhaigou. Water so clear and colorful it looks like a painting. The lakebed is covered in fallen tree trunks and calcium deposits that create the blue-green-gold patterns. This is the spot for the iconic Jiuzhaigou photo.

**Pearl Shoal Waterfall (珍珠滩瀑布)** — Wide, shallow water cascading over a limestone滩. It's where the opening scene of *Journey to the West* was filmed. Yes, that famous waterfall is here.

**Mirror Lake (镜海)** — On a still morning, the reflection is so perfect you can't tell which way is up. Visit before 10 AM when the wind picks up.

- 📍 Jiuzhaigou National Park, Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture
- ⏰ 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM (entry stops at 2 PM)
- 💰 ¥250 / ~$34 (peak season) + bus ¥90 / ~$12
- 🕐 Full day inside the park

**Tip:** Bring a rain jacket. Jiuzhaigou weather changes fast. I got caught in a downpour at 11 AM, sunburn at 2 PM, and fog by 4 PM.

### Day 6: Jiuzhaigou — The Blue Dream (Part 2)

![Jiuzhaigou Valley](/images/jiuzhaigou/jiuzhaigou_waterfall_forest_scenic_park_nature_003.webp)


**Theme:** Waterfalls, villages, and hidden corners

#### Shuzheng Valley (树正沟)

Today explore the entrance valley. **Shuzheng Waterfall** (树正瀑布) and **Shuzheng Lakes** (树正群海) are stunning — a series of 19 small lakes cascading down like a staircase. The Tibetan villages nestled between the lakes add a human element that the upper valleys lack.

#### Nuorilang Grassland

In summer, the grassland blooms with wildflowers. It's a nice break from the lakes and waterfalls — just open meadow with mountains on all sides.

#### Tibetan Culture

Jiuzhaigou is home to Tibetan and Qiang communities. If you're lucky, you might stumble upon a village festival or prayer flag ceremony. Respect local customs — don't touch prayer flags, don't point at religious symbols, and always walk clockwise around mani stone piles.

#### Evening: Last Night Near Jiuzhaigou

Dinner at a Tibetan restaurant in Zhangzha Town. Try ** yak butter tea** (酥油茶) — it's an acquired taste, but you're in Tibet-adjacent territory, so embrace it.

---

### Day 7: Jiuzhaigou → Chengdu → Departure

**Theme:** Long journey home

#### Early Morning: Last Walk

If you have time before your transport, take a short walk along the Zhangzha River. The water is milky blue from glacial runoff — even outside the park, Jiuzhaigou's beauty spills over.

#### Return to Chengdu

- **Flight:** Jiuhuang Airport → Chengdu, 45 minutes. Book early — flights can fill up
- **Bus:** 8–10 hours, not recommended for Day 7 if you have evening commitments

#### If You Have Time in Chengdu

Head to **Yulin Road** (玉林路) for a final meal. This neighborhood is where the song "Chengdu" (成都 by Zhao Lei) was written. Find a small restaurant, order some串串 (skewers), and raise a glass to one of the best weeks of your life.

## 💡 Practical Tips

1. **Altitude sickness** — Jiuzhaigou ranges from 2,000–3,100m. Bring ibuprofen, stay hydrated, don't overexert yourself on Day 4.
2. **Weather in Jiuzhaigou** — Bring layers. Temperature can swing 15°C in a single day. Rain gear is essential.
3. **Book Jiuzhaigou tickets online** — The park has a daily visitor cap. Book at least 3 days in advance at [www.jiuzhai.com](http://www.jiuzhai.com).
4. **Don't feed the fish** — Jiuzhaigou's fish are protected. ¥500 fine if caught feeding them.
5. **Photography tips** — Polarizing filter for the lakes. Overcast days actually give better water color than sunny days (less glare).
6. **Pack snacks for inside the park** — Food inside is expensive and limited. Bring bread, fruit, and water.
