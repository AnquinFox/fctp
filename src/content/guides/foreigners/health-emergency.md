# 🏥 Health & Emergency Guide for Foreigners in China (2026)

![Hospital](/images/general/health-hospital.webp)

## Staying Safe in China

China is generally very safe for travelers. Here's what you need to know about health and emergencies.

---

## 🆘 Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Police** | **110** | For crimes, accidents |
| **Ambulance** | **120** | Medical emergencies |
| **Fire** | **119** | Fire emergencies |
| **Traffic accident** | **122** | Road accidents |
| **Foreigner hotline** | **12367** | Immigration queries |

> ⚠️ **Save these numbers in your phone immediately!**

- **Chinese pharmacies** — may not have Western brands

- **Prescription meds** — bring a doctor's note in English

## 🍽️ Food Safety

### General Rules:

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Eat at busy restaurants** | Fresh food = popular place |
| **Avoid raw vegetables** | May not be properly washed |
| **Drink bottled water** | Tap water not safe to drink |
| **Be cautious with ice** | May be made from tap water |
| **Eat hot food** | Freshly cooked is safest |

### Common Food Issues:

| Issue | Prevention | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Stomach upset** | Avoid street food initially | Pepto-Bismol, rest |
| **Food poisoning** | Eat at reputable places | Seek medical help |
| **Allergies** | Learn Chinese allergen words | Antihistamines |

## 🔒 Safety Tips

![Emergency Services](/images/general/health-emergency.webp)

### General Safety:

| Tip | Details |
|-----|---------|
| **China is very safe** | Low crime rate compared to most countries |
| **Watch for scams** | Be cautious of overly friendly strangers |
| **Keep valuables secure** | Use hotel safe, don't flash cash |
| **Avoid demonstrations** | Stay away from political gatherings |
| **Register with embassy** | Let your country know you're in China |

### Common Scams:

| Scam | How to Avoid |
|------|-------------|
| **Tea ceremony scam** | Politely decline invitations from strangers |
| **Art student scam** | Don't follow strangers to "art exhibitions" |
| **Fake police** | Real police won't ask for your wallet |
| **Taxi scam** | Use Didi app, insist on meter |
| **Bar scam** | Don't follow strangers to bars |

### Night Safety:

- **Well-lit streets** — stick to main roads at night
- **Public transport** — metro runs until ~11pm, buses later
- **Hotel area** — stay near your accommodation
- **Phone charged** — keep battery for emergencies

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Buy travel insurance before departure** — it's too late once you arrive
2. **Keep emergency numbers saved** — in phone and written down
3. **Carry a medical card** — with blood type, allergies, emergency contact
4. **Bring essential medicines** — from your home country
5. **Learn basic medical Chinese** — for emergencies
6. **Stay hydrated** — drink bottled water only
7. **Rest when sick** — Chinese hospitals can be overwhelming
