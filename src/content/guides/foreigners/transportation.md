## Getting Around China

China has the world's most modern transportation infrastructure. Here's how to use it.

---

## 🚇 Metro (Subway)

China's metro systems are **fast, clean, affordable, and foreigner-friendly**.

### Cities with Metro:

| City | Lines | Foreigner-Friendly |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| **Shanghai** | 20+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |
| **Beijing** | 27+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |
| **Guangzhou** | 16+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |
| **Chengdu** | 13+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |
| **Shenzhen** | 16+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |
| **Hangzhou** | 12+ | ✅ English signage, top-up machines |

### How to Use Metro:

1. **Enter station**: Tap card or scan QR code at gate
2. **Navigate**: Follow English signs to your line
3. **Board train**: Stand behind the yellow line
4. **Exit station**: Tap card or scan QR code at gate

### Payment Options:

| Method | How to Use | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Transport card** | Buy at station, top up with cash | Frequent riders |
| **WeChat/Alipay QR** | Scan at gate | Convenience |
| **Single trip ticket** | Buy from machine | One-time rides |

### Transport Card (Recommended):

- **Where to buy**: Metro station ticket machines
- **Cost**: ¥20 deposit + top-up amount
- **Where to top up**: Station machines (cash only)
- **Refund**: Return card at any station, get deposit back
- **Cities**: Some cards work across multiple cities (Yangtze Delta region)

## 🚄 High-Speed Rail (HSR)

China's high-speed rail network is the **world's largest and most advanced**.

### Key Routes:

| Route | Distance | Time | Price |
|-------|----------|------|-------|
| **Beijing → Shanghai** | 1,318 km | 4.5 hours | ¥553 ($75) |
| **Beijing → Guangzhou** | 2,300 km | 8 hours | ¥862 ($118) |
| **Shanghai → Hangzhou** | 169 km | 45 min | ¥73 ($10) |
| **Chengdu → Chongqing** | 320 km | 1.5 hours | ¥154 ($21) |

### How to Book Tickets:

#### Option 1: 12306 App (Recommended)

1. Download **铁路12306** app
2. Register with **passport number**
3. Search for your route
4. Select train and seat class
5. Pay with **Alipay or WeChat Pay**
6. Show **QR code or passport** at station

#### Option 2: Trip.com

1. Open **Trip.com** app
2. Search for train routes
3. Book with **international credit card**
4. Collect ticket at station with passport

#### Option 3: Station Ticket Window

1. Go to station **ticket window**
2. Show your **passport**
3. Pay with **cash or card**
4. Get paper ticket

### Seat Classes:

| Class | Price | Comfort | Best For |
|-------|-------|---------|----------|
| **Second Class** | 1x | Basic seating | Budget travel |
| **First Class** | 1.5x | More legroom | Comfort |
| **Business Class** | 3x | Lie-flat seats | Luxury |

### Station Tips:

- **Arrive 30 minutes early** — security checks take time
- **Passport required** — for ticket purchase and boarding
- **English signs** — available at major stations
- **Free WiFi** — available at most stations
- **Food courts** — available at all major stations

## 🚕 Taxis

Taxis are **widely available** in all major cities.

### How to Get a Taxi:

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Street hail** | Easy | May be refused by busy drivers |
| **Hotel taxi stand** | Reliable | Slightly more expensive |
| **Didi app** | Best option | Requires app setup |

### Didi (Recommended):

Didi is China's Uber — **easier to use than hailing a taxi**.

1. Open **Didi** app
2. Enter destination (in Chinese or English)
3. See **price estimate** before booking
4. Pay with **WeChat/Alipay**
5. Rate your driver

### Taxi Tips:

- **Always use the meter** — or agree on price before getting in
- **Have your destination written in Chinese** — show to driver
- **Keep small bills** — drivers may not have change for large notes
- **Most taxis don't accept cards** — cash or mobile payment only
- **Tipping not expected** — but appreciated

## 🚲 Bike Sharing

Bike sharing is **everywhere** in Chinese cities.

### Major Services:

| Service | How to Use | Price |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **Meituan Bike (美团单车)** | Scan QR with Meituan app | ¥1.5/30min |
| **Hello Bike (哈啰单车)** | Scan QR with Hello app | ¥1.5/30min |
| **Didi Bike** | Scan QR with Didi app | ¥1.5/30min |

### How to Use:

1. Open **Meituan/Hello/Didi** app
2. Scan the **QR code** on the bike
3. **Unlock** the bike
4. Ride to your destination
5. **Park** in a designated area
6. **Lock** the bike manually

> 💡 **Pro tip**: Park in designated zones or you'll be charged extra!

## 🚌 Bus

Buses are the **most affordable** way to get around.

### How to Use:

1. **Wait at bus stop** — check route number
2. **Board bus** — tap card or scan QR at front door
3. **Find a seat** — or stand and hold on
4. **Exit bus** — tap card or scan QR at back door

### Payment:

- **Transport card** — same as metro card
- **WeChat/Alipay QR** — scan at front door
- **Cash** — exact change only (usually ¥1-3)

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Get a transport card** — saves time and works on metro + bus
2. **Use Didi for taxis** — more reliable than street hailing
3. **Book train tickets early** — popular routes sell out
4. **Learn your station name in Chinese** — for taxi drivers
5. **Download offline maps** — in case internet fails
6. **Carry a portable charger** — mobile payment + maps = heavy usage
7. **Keep your passport with you** — needed for train tickets and some metro stations
