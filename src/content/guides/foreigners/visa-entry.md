## Do You Need a Visa?

China has **greatly expanded** visa-free policies. Whether you need a visa depends on your nationality and planned stay.

---

## ✅ Visa-Free Entry (50+ Countries)

Citizens of **50+ countries** can enter mainland China **without any visa** for tourism, family visits, or sightseeing for **up to 30 days**.

### Eligible Countries (Key Ones):

| Region | Countries |
|--------|-----------|
| **Europe (35+)** | France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Switzerland, Sweden, Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Norway, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Greece, Poland, Portugal, Hungary, Czech Republic, UK, and more |
| **Asia-Pacific (7)** | Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Australia, New Zealand, Brunei, Malaysia |
| **Americas (3)** | United States, Canada, Brazil |

> ⚠️ **Always check the latest list** on the National Immigration Administration (NIA) website before travel: [https://www.nia.gov.cn](https://www.nia.gov.cn)

---

## ⏰ 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit (New in 2026!)

Citizens of **55 countries** can transit through China **visa-free for up to 240 hours (10 days)** at **60+ designated ports**.

### How It Works:

1. You must have a **confirmed onward ticket** to a third country/region
2. You can enter through any of the **60+ designated ports** (major cities)
3. You can travel within the **designated transit area** (usually the province/city + neighboring areas)

### Key Requirements:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Eligible nationalities** | 55 countries (most Western nations included) |
| **Maximum stay** | 240 hours (10 days) from entry |
| **Must have** | Confirmed onward ticket to a third country |
| **Entry ports** | 60+ designated ports (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, etc.) |
| **Transit area** | Usually province-level region + adjacent areas |

> 💡 This is a great option for **stopover tourism** — explore China during a layover!

## 🛂 Visa Application (If Needed)

### Visa Types for Tourists:

| Visa Type | Duration | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **L Visa** | 30-90 days | Tourism |
| **M Visa** | 30-90 days | Business |
| **F Visa** | 30-90 days | Short-term visit/exchange |
| **Transit (G)** | 72-240 hours | Transit through China |

### How to Apply:

1. **Gather required documents** (see checklist below)
2. **Apply at a Chinese embassy/consulate** or through an authorized visa service
3. **Processing time**: 4-7 business days (standard), 2-3 days (express)
4. **Visa fees**: Vary by nationality and type ($30-$140 USD typically)

### Required Documents Checklist:

- [ ] **Passport** with 6+ months validity and blank pages
- [ ] **Visa application form** (completed online at [https://www.visaforchina.org](https://www.visaforchina.org))
- [ ] **Recent passport photo** (48mm × 33mm, white background)
- [ ] **Proof of travel**: flight itinerary, hotel reservations
- [ ] **Proof of financial means**: bank statements (last 3-6 months)
- [ ] **Travel insurance** covering China

## 📋 Arrival Procedures

### At the Airport:

1. **Immigration Check**: Have your passport and visa (or visa-free eligibility proof) ready
2. **Customs Declaration**: Fill out the customs declaration form (or use the electronic kiosk)
3. **Baggage Claim**: Collect your luggage
4. **Currency Exchange**: Available at the airport (but see payment guide for better options)

### Documents to Keep Handy:

| Document | Why You Need It |
|----------|----------------|
| **Passport** | ID everywhere in China |
| **Visa** (if applicable) | Immigration checks |
| **Hotel confirmation** | Some hotels require it |
| **Return flight ticket** | Immigration may ask for it |
| **Travel insurance** | Medical emergencies |

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Register within 24 hours**: You must register your accommodation with local police. Hotels do this automatically; if staying with friends, you must visit the local police station.
2. **Keep your passport with you**: You'll need it for hotel check-in, train tickets, and sometimes random checks.
3. **Download the NIA app**: "国家移民管理局" app for visa-free entry status and immigration queries.
4. **Proof of onward travel**: For visa-free transit, keep your onward ticket easily accessible.
