# 🌿 Yangtze River Delta Elegance
> : Shanghai → Hangzhou → Suzhou

> 📅 July 2026 | 📍 Culture seekers & garden lovers | ⏱️ 5 Days / 4 Nights

The Yangtze River Delta is where China's refined side lives. Silk, gardens, tea plantations, and a literary tradition stretching back a thousand years. If Beijing is China's brain and Shanghai its ambition, the Yangtze Delta is its soul.

I designed this trip around the idea of "slow travel" — each city has a different tempo. Shanghai buzzes, Hangzhou breathes, Suzhou whispers. Five days to feel all three.

## 📅 Itinerary

---

### Day 1: Shanghai — Arrival & The Old Shanghai

![Shanghai skyline](/images/shanghai/shanghai_bund_night_002.webp)


**Theme:** Before the bullet trains took over, Shanghai was already magic

#### Yu Garden (豫园)

A 400-year-old classical garden in the heart of the city. Every rock, every pond, every pavilion was designed to create a microcosm of nature. The Exquisite Jade Rock (玉玲珑) is the centerpiece — a porous limestone boulder with 72 holes that release incense smoke from every opening.

- 📍 Address: 218 Anren Street, Huangpu District
- 🚇 Metro Line 10, Yuyuan Garden Station
- ⏰ 9:00 AM – 4:30 PM
- 💰 ¥40 / ~$5.50

**Lunch:** Eat at **Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant** (南翔馒头店) in the Yu Garden bazaar. The xiaolongbao here are ¥30–50 per serving. Yes, there's a queue. Yes, it's worth it.

#### The Bund (外滩)

Walk the Bund from south to north. 52 buildings along 1.5km representing Gothic, Baroque, Romanesque, Art Deco, and Chinese Renaissance styles. Each building tells the story of a different trading nation that shaped old Shanghai.

Best time: Late afternoon into sunset. Watch the Lujiazui skyline light up across the river as the colonial buildings glow in golden hour.

- 💰 Free

#### Nanjing Road (南京路)

Walk from the Bund westward along China's most famous shopping street. It's neon-lit and commercial, but the architecture is worth looking up at. Turn off at any side street for quieter, more interesting finds.

🛏️ **Stay near**: Nanjing East Road or People's Square — central for everything

### Day 2: Shanghai → Hangzhou — West Lake by Bike

**Theme:** The most famous lake in China

#### Morning: Train to Hangzhou

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Route | Shanghai Hongqiao → Hangzhou East |
| Duration | ~45 minutes |
| Price | ¥75–130 / ~$10–18 |
| Frequency | Every 10–15 minutes |

#### West Lake (西湖)

UNESCO World Heritage Site, and one of the most romantic places in China. The "Ten Scenes of West Lake" have been celebrated in poetry for centuries. Rent a bike and ride the 15km loop around the lake — it's flat, scenic, and there are tea houses every few hundred meters.

**Highlights along the loop:**
- **Su Causeway** (苏堤) — Built by poet Su Dongpo in 1089. Willow trees line both sides
- **Broken Bridge** (断桥) — The most famous bridge in Chinese literature. Legend says it's where a white snake spirit fell in love with a mortal
- **Three Pools Mirroring the Moon** (三潭印月) — Three stone pagodas in the middle of the lake. On Mid-Autumn Festival, candles inside each pagoda reflect 33 moons on the water

- 📍 Hangzhou, West Lake Scenic Area
- 💰 Free (boat ride ¥55 / ~$7.60)
- 🕐 Spend 3–4 hours cycling

#### Longjing Tea Village (龙井村)

After the lake, ride or Didi to Longjing village. This is where the world's most famous green tea is grown. Sit at a family-run tea house, taste fresh-picked Dragon Well tea, and watch the tea terraces roll down the hillsides.

**Tip:** Buy tea directly from farmers (¥100–300 / ~$14–41 per 50g for first flush). Tourist shops charge triple.

🛏️ **Stay near**: West Lake area — Hefang Street or Nanshan Road for walkability

### Day 3: Hangzhou — Temples, Silk & Night Market

![Lingyin Temple, Hangzhou](/images/hangzhou/hangzhou_jiuxi_stream_002.webp)


**Theme:** Spiritual morning, sensory evening

#### Lingyin Temple (灵隐寺)

One of the largest Buddhist temples in China, founded in 328 AD. The main hall is enormous — the reclining Buddha inside is 24 meters long. The surrounding forest is filled with carved stone Buddhas dating back to the Song Dynasty.

- 📍 Address: 1 Fayun Road, Xihu District
- ⏰ 7:00 AM – 5:30 PM
- 💰 ¥75 / ~$10.40 (includes Fei Lai Feng entrance)
- 🕐 1.5–2 hours

**My advice:** Go at opening time. By 10 AM, tour groups arrive in waves and the peaceful atmosphere vanishes.

#### China National Silk Museum

Hangzhou was the center of the ancient Silk Road's production. This museum tells the whole story — from silkworm to fabric. The dyeing demonstrations are surprisingly fascinating.

- 📍 Address: 73-1 Yuhuangshan Road
- ⏰ 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Mondays)
- 💰 Free

#### Hefang Street (河坊街)

Evening market street with food stalls, traditional medicine shops, and craft vendors. Try **ding shi bing** (定胜饼, victory cakes) — a Hangzhou specialty with a sweet red bean filling. ¥5 per cake.

### Day 4: Hangzhou → Suzhou — Gardens & Canals

![Jiuxi Stream](/images/hangzhou/hangzhou_jiuxi_stream_002.webp)


**Theme:** Chinese garden art at its peak

#### Morning: Train to Suzhou

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Route | Hangzhou East → Suzhou North |
| Duration | ~1.5 hours |
| Price | ¥120 / ~$17 |
| Frequency | Every 20–30 minutes |

#### Humble Administrator's Garden (拙政园)

The largest and most famous classical garden in China. Built in 1509, it's a masterwork of Chinese landscape design — every view is a carefully composed painting. The garden covers 5.2 hectares with 31 pavilions, 18 bridges, and countless rockeries.

The genius of Suzhou gardens is that they create "borrowed views" — each window and doorway frames a different scene. You're always seeing the next space through a circular moon gate or a hexagonal window.

- 📍 Address: 178 Northeast Street, Gusu District
- 🚇 Metro Line 4, Beisi Ta Station
- ⏰ 7:30 AM – 5:30 PM
- 💰 ¥80 / ~$11 (peak season)

**Lunch:** Walk to **Tong De Xing** (同得兴) for Suzhou-style noodles. The pork rib noodle soup is legendary. ¥25–35 / ~$3.50–5.

#### Pingjiang Road (平江路)

A 800-year-old canal-side street. Old whitewashed houses line the water, bridges connect both sides, and Kunqu opera performers occasionally sing from the teahouses. This is Suzhou at its most atmospheric.

- 💰 Free to walk
- 🕐 1–2 hours of wandering

#### Evening: Jinji Lake (金鸡湖)

Modern Suzhou's showpiece. The lakefront has a massive music fountain, contemporary art installations, and the tallest building in Suzhou (the Gate to the East, looking like a giant pair of pants). Dinner at one of the lakeside restaurants — try Suzhou-style **squirrel fish** (松鼠桂鱼), a sweet-and-sour whole fish that looks like a squirrel's tail.

🛏️ **Stay near**: Pingjiang Road area or Guanqian Street — traditional neighborhood

---

### Day 5: Suzhou — Tiger Hill & Departure

![Suzhou water town](/images/suzhou/suzhou_water_town_cover_009.webp)


**Theme:** Last morning in the garden city

#### Tiger Hill (虎丘)

Called "the most famous hill in the Wu region" by Su Dongpo. The leaning Cloud Rock Pagoda (云岩寺塔) has been tilting for 400 years — China's version of Pisa. The Sword Pool (剑池) legend says a king's sword was hidden here.

- 📍 Address: 314虎丘山门内8号, Gusu District
- ⏰ 7:30 AM – 6:00 PM
- 💰 ¥60 / ~$8.30

#### Suzhou Museum

Right next to the Humble Administrator's Garden. Designed by I.M. Pei (who grew up in Suzhou) — the building itself is a masterpiece. Modern geometry meets traditional Chinese architecture. Free admission.

- 🚇 Metro Line 4, Beisi Ta Station
- ⏰ 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Mondays)
- 💰 Free

#### Lunch: Suzhou Mooncakes

Not the traditional Mid-Autumn kind — Suzhou has a year-round savory mooncake (苏式月饼) with flaky pastry and meat filling. **Huang Tianyuan** (黄天源) has been making them since 1821. ¥5–8 each.

#### Departure

Suzhou is perfectly positioned between Shanghai (30 min by train) and Nanjing (1 hour). Fly out of Shanghai Pudong or Hongqiao airports, or continue to your next destination.

## 💡 Practical Tips

1. **Train tickets are easy** — Shanghai↔Hangzhou and Hangzhou↔Suzhou are frequent and cheap. No need to book more than 1 day ahead.
2. **Suzhou gardens are small but detailed** — Don't rush. Sit in the pavilions and watch how light plays on the water.
3. **West Lake is best at dawn or dusk** — Midday is hot and crowded. Early morning cycling is magical.
4. **Tea buying** — If you buy Longjing tea, taste before buying. Good Longjing is flat, smooth, and has a sweet aftertaste. Bad Longjing is bitter and curled.
5. **Suzhou Museum closes on Mondays** — Like most museums in China. Plan accordingly.
6. **Rainy days are fine** — Suzhou gardens are arguably MORE beautiful in the rain. The misty atmosphere is authentic.
