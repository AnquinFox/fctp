## 馃搶 Read Before You Go

Before coming to Shanghai, you only need to know one thing: **Don't join a tour group, don't go to Nanjing Road, don't eat at tourist attractions.**

All the cool stuff in Shanghai is tucked away in the little plane-tree-lined streets. This itinerary was planned by a local friend of mine, and I walked it for three days without a single dud.

Use the metro for transport. Download Gaode Maps (Amap) 鈥?that's all you need. Shanghai has the densest metro network in China, making everywhere accessible.

### Wukang Road

![Wukang Road scene](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/shanghai_tower_gw.webp)

Start at the Wukang Building and walk south. This road is SO photogenic 鈥?every corner is a scene.

Make sure to photograph the Wukang Building 鈥?that famous viral corner. Go around 3-4 PM for the best light. Stand at the northeast corner of the intersection to get both the building and the plane trees in frame.

> 鈿狅笍 On holidays, the Wukang intersection is completely packed. Come on a weekday or arrive before 9 AM.

Walk from Wukang Road to Anfu Road (about 15 minutes). The whole way is lined with caf茅s and concept stores. Just wander.

### Anfu Road

Anfu Road is where Shanghai's best-dressed people shop. It's short, but every shop is worth stepping into.

**Lunch** recommendation: `% Arabica` (trendy coffee) or `RAC Bar` (brunch at its finest). Both are on Anfu Road, a 2-minute walk apart.

## 鉁?Day 2锝淔rench Concession City Walk 鈫?Yu Garden 鈫?The Bund

**Today is a walking day 鈥?wear comfortable shoes.**

### French Concession City Walk

Start from Xintiandi and walk this route: Fuxing Road 鈫?South Maoming Road 鈫?Sinan Road 鈫?Fuxing Park.

This is Shanghai's most pleasant walking route. French plane trees and old villas line both sides 鈥?quiet, atmospheric, perfect.

![French Concession plane trees](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/lujiazui_gw.webp)
*The French Concession's tree shade and old villas 鈥?comfortable even in summer*

Along the way you'll pass:
- **Sinan Mansions**: a cluster of old villas, incredibly photogenic
- **Fuxing Park**: Shanghai's oldest French-style park 鈥?sit down and relax
- **Zhou Enlai Residence**: free entry

### Yu Garden (Afternoon)

Metro Line 10 to Yuyuan Station.

Yu Garden itself is a classic Jiangnan garden. 楼40 admission, worth going in. But the main attraction nearby is the **Chenghuang Temple Food Street** 鈥?not for eating, but for soaking up the lively atmosphere.

> 馃挕 Don't eat inside Chenghuang Temple 鈥?overpriced and mediocre. For authentic food, head to Laoximen or Changli Road.

![Yu Garden](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/yuyuan_bridge_night_gw.webp)
*Yu Garden's Nine-Turn Bridge 鈥?worth seeing both day and night*

### The Bund (Evening)

Metro Line 2 to East Nanjing Road Station, then a 10-minute walk to the Bund.

The Bund is a MUST at night. It's scorching hot during the day and jam-packed in the evening, but that half-hour around sunset is pure gold.

Walk along the riverside and watch the lights of Lujiazui flicker on one by one. The Oriental Pearl, Shanghai Tower, and SWFC 鈥?all three skyscrapers across the river.

![The Bund night view](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/bund_collage_night_gw.webp)
*The Bund's heritage architecture and Lujiazui's skyline across the river 鈥?Shanghai's classic image*

> 鈿狅笍 Don't try to catch a taxi here. The traffic will drive you crazy. Take the metro.

**Day 2 Accommodation**: Switch to a hotel near the Bund if your budget allows 鈥?a room with a river view is something else.
