## 📌 Read Before You Go

Before coming to Shanghai, you only need to know one thing: **Don't join a tour group, don't go to Nanjing Road, don't eat at tourist attractions.**

All the cool stuff in Shanghai is tucked away in the little plane-tree-lined streets. This itinerary was planned by a local friend of mine, and I walked it for three days without a single dud.

Use the metro for transport. Download Gaode Maps (Amap) — that's all you need. Shanghai has the densest metro network in China, making everywhere accessible.

---

## ✨ Day 1｜Wukang Road → Anfu Road → Xintiandi

**Arrive in Shanghai in the afternoon. Take Metro Line 10/11 to Jiaotong University Station directly.**

No sightseeing on Day 1. Just walk the streets.

### Wukang Road

![Wukang Road scene](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/shanghai_tower_gw.jpg)
*Wukang Road's plane trees and old villas — every shot is a masterpiece*

Start at the Wukang Building and walk south. This road is SO photogenic — every corner is a scene.

Make sure to photograph the Wukang Building — that famous viral corner. Go around 3-4 PM for the best light. Stand at the northeast corner of the intersection to get both the building and the plane trees in frame.

> ⚠️ On holidays, the Wukang intersection is completely packed. Come on a weekday or arrive before 9 AM.

Walk from Wukang Road to Anfu Road (about 15 minutes). The whole way is lined with cafés and concept stores. Just wander.

### Anfu Road

Anfu Road is where Shanghai's best-dressed people shop. It's short, but every shop is worth stepping into.

**Lunch** recommendation: `% Arabica` (trendy coffee) or `RAC Bar` (brunch at its finest). Both are on Anfu Road, a 2-minute walk apart.

![Shanghai street scene](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/happyvalley_gw.jpg)
*Shanghai's plane trees and the laid-back street vibe*

### Xintiandi

In the evening, take the metro or taxi to Xintiandi. Everything here — food, drink, shopping — is solid. Hard to go wrong.

I'd recommend the Thai place next to % Arabica (around ¥120 per person), or `Old Jackie's` (老吉士) for Shanghainese food (about ¥150 per person, reservation required).

![Xintiandi night scene](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/xintiandi_shikumen_gw.jpg)
*Xintiandi's shikumen architecture and neon lights — a must for Shanghai nightlife*

**Day 1 Accommodation**: Stay near Xintiandi or Middle Huaihai Road — convenient for Day 2's French Concession exploration.

## ✨ Day 2｜French Concession City Walk → Yu Garden → The Bund

**Today is a walking day — wear comfortable shoes.**

### French Concession City Walk

Start from Xintiandi and walk this route: Fuxing Road → South Maoming Road → Sinan Road → Fuxing Park.

This is Shanghai's most pleasant walking route. French plane trees and old villas line both sides — quiet, atmospheric, perfect.

![French Concession plane trees](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/lujiazui_gw.jpg)
*The French Concession's tree shade and old villas — comfortable even in summer*

Along the way you'll pass:
- **Sinan Mansions**: a cluster of old villas, incredibly photogenic
- **Fuxing Park**: Shanghai's oldest French-style park — sit down and relax
- **Zhou Enlai Residence**: free entry

### Yu Garden (Afternoon)

Metro Line 10 to Yuyuan Station.

Yu Garden itself is a classic Jiangnan garden. ¥40 admission, worth going in. But the main attraction nearby is the **Chenghuang Temple Food Street** — not for eating, but for soaking up the lively atmosphere.

> 💡 Don't eat inside Chenghuang Temple — overpriced and mediocre. For authentic food, head to Laoximen or Changli Road.

![Yu Garden](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/yuyuan_bridge_night_gw.jpg)
*Yu Garden's Nine-Turn Bridge — worth seeing both day and night*

### The Bund (Evening)

Metro Line 2 to East Nanjing Road Station, then a 10-minute walk to the Bund.

The Bund is a MUST at night. It's scorching hot during the day and jam-packed in the evening, but that half-hour around sunset is pure gold.

Walk along the riverside and watch the lights of Lujiazui flicker on one by one. The Oriental Pearl, Shanghai Tower, and SWFC — all three skyscrapers across the river.

![The Bund night view](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/bund_collage_night_gw.jpg)
*The Bund's heritage architecture and Lujiazui's skyline across the river — Shanghai's classic image*

> ⚠️ Don't try to catch a taxi here. The traffic will drive you crazy. Take the metro.

**Day 2 Accommodation**: Switch to a hotel near the Bund if your budget allows — a room with a river view is something else.

## ✨ Day 3｜Disney (Optional) / Zhujiajiao Water Town / Wrap-up

Two choices for Day 3, depending on your energy and interest:

### Option A: Disneyland (Full Day)

If you're going to Disney, this whole day belongs to it. Take Metro Line 11 straight there. Leave at 7:30 AM.

Tickets: ¥475 (weekdays) / ¥599 (weekends). Buy in advance on the app.

![Disney Castle](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/disney_castle_night_gw.jpg)
*Disney Castle — every girl's dream, honestly*

### Option B: Zhujiajiao Water Town (Half Day)

If Disney isn't your thing, head to Zhujiajiao. Metro Line 17 direct, 1 hour away.

It's an authentic water town — way fewer tourists than Wuzhen, much less commercialized. Take a boat ride, eat zongzi (rice dumplings), wander the old streets.

![Zhujiajiao](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/pearl_tower_multiangle_gw.jpg)
*Zhujiajiao's Fangsheng Bridge — a quiet corner of Jiangnan water town*

In the afternoon, stop by Nanjing Road to pick up some souvenirs. Then pack up and head to the airport or train station.

## 🍜 No-Trap Food List

Shanghai food, in two words: **refined and precise**. Small portions, beautiful presentation, careful preparation.

| Dish | Recommended | Cost | Location |
|------|-------------|------|----------|
| Shanghainese braised pork | Old Jackie's (老吉士) | ¥150 | Tianping Road |
| Pan-fried buns | Xiao Yang Shengjian (小杨生煎) | ¥25 | Multiple branches |
| Scallion oil noodles | A Da Scallion Pancake (阿大葱油饼) | ¥10 | South Maoming Road |
| Soup dumplings | Nanxiang Steamed Bun (南翔馒头店) | ¥30 | Yu Garden |
| Crab roe noodles | Chenglonghang (成隆行) | ¥200 | West Jianguo Road |

> 💡 Eating out in Shanghai is generally pricier than other Chinese cities, but the quality is always there.

## 🚇 Transport Guide

**Airports**: Pudong (mostly international) / Hongqiao (mostly domestic)
- Pudong to city: Maglev (8 min to Longyang Road, ¥50) + metro
- Hongqiao to city: Metro Lines 2/10 direct

**City**: Metro + cabs
- Metro: covers all attractions, ¥3-7 per ride
- Taxi: starting fare ¥16, typically ¥20-40 within the city center
- Don't drive! Shanghai parking is expensive and the traffic is brutal.

## ⚠️ Trap-Avoidance Guide

1. **Don't go to Nanjing Road** — pedestrian streets look the same in every Chinese city, waste of time
2. **Don't eat at tourist attractions** — Chenghuang Temple and Bund-area restaurants are overpriced and mediocre
3. **Don't take a taxi to the Bund** — traffic will make you question your life choices
4. **Don't visit in summer** — July-August hits 40°C; winter is actually more comfortable
5. **Don't join a tour group** — Shanghai is for slow wandering. A rushed tour bus experience misses everything
