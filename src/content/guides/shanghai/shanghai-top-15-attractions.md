## 🥇 #1: The Bund (Exposition of World Architecture)

![The Bund](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/bund_panorama_daytime_gw.webp)

**The undisputed champion.** The Bund stretches along Zhongshan Dong Yi Road, facing the Huangpu River with Lujiazui in the distance. A whole row of buildings in every imaginable style from the former concession era —Baroque, Romanesque, Neoclassical, Gothic, Renaissance —earning it the name "Exposition of World Architecture."

> 🚶 **My route**: Walk south along the Bund to Jinling Road Pier, spend 楼2 on a ferry to the opposite bank's riverside promenade —affordable AND a great river experience!
> 🚇 **Metro**: Line 2, East Nanjing Road Station →?walk along Nanjing Road to the Peace Hotel →?start your Bund journey

- 📸 Must-photo: Waibaidu Bridge (100+ years of history, classic *Shanghai Bund* scene)
- 🌃 Night view: Lights on daily, off at 22:00

## 🥉 #3: Lujiazui Skyscrapers

![Lujiazui](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/tianzifang_alley_gw.webp)

Lujiazui is Shanghai's financial center —over a hundred towers, one of the world's densest concentrations of super-tall buildings. The four giants:

| Rank | Tower | Height |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Shanghai Tower | 632m |
| 2 | SWFC (World Financial Center) | 492m |
| 3 | Jin Mao Tower | 420m |
| 4 | Oriental Pearl Tower | 468m |

> 💡 If you don't want to go sky-high, check out the Shanghai History Museum at the base of the Oriental Pearl —wax figures and models trace the city's development.

## #4: Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street

![Nanjing Road](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/zhujiajiao_gw.webp)

The street that best captures Shanghai's "Ten-Mile Foreign Settlement" vibe, with over a century of history. The four great department stores of the 1930s (Sincere, Wing On, Sun Sun, Da Sun) started the commercial boom here. Now it's been upgraded to "version 3.0" —century-old shops + classic department stores + flagship brands + anime & 2D culture malls + Miniso Land!

> 🚶 **My route**: Start at People's Square and walk east. You'll hit the Peace Hotel at the end, connecting to the Bund.
> 🚇 **Metro**: Lines 1/2/8, People's Square Station, Exit 7

## #6: Yu Garden

![Yu Garden](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/disney_castle_night_gw.webp)

"The most Chinese place in all of Shanghai." Yu Garden is a Ming Dynasty manor house with rock gardens, carved beams, and classic Jiangnan garden features. Highlights include the **Dianchun Hall** (site of the Small Sword Society Uprising) and the **Exquisite Jade Rock** —one of the three legendary rocks of Jiangnan. The Old Chenghuang Temple dates back to the Ming Dynasty's Yongle era —"one temple, two city gods" is rare nationwide.

> 🍜 **Food**: Nanxiang Steamed Bun, Green Wave Gallery, Da Hu Chun
> 🚇 **Metro**: Lines 10/14, Yuyuan Station, Exit 7

## #7: Xujiahui Cathedral

"The greatest cathedral in the Far East." Construction began in 1905, completed in 1910. Its solemn Gothic exterior is so beautiful it's been featured in countless TV dramas and movies (including *Fated to Love You* and *Empire of the Sun*).

> 📚 The adjacent **Xujiahui Academy** was designed by David Chipperfield Architects —the latest Instagram hotspot
> 🚇 **Metro**: Lines 1/9/11, Xujiahui Station, Exit 3

## #8: Suzhou Creek Riverside Walk

Walk along the Suzhou Creek waterfront and you'll be greeted by historic building after historic building: Du Yuesheng's private granary, the former Fuxin Flour Mill, **Sihang Warehouse**, the Embankment Building, Shanghai Postal Museum, Waibaidu Bridge, China Securities Museum—There are even dedicated sightseeing boats on the creek now!

---

## #9: Longhua Temple & Jade Buddha Temple

- **Longhua Temple**: Its history stretches back to the Three Kingdoms (247 AD) —a thousand-year-old temple. With the new metro line, the whole area got a facelift —now you can pray AND visit a mega shopping mall. The temple grounds have many friendly cats 🐱
- **Jade Buddha Temple**: In the late Qing Dynasty, Master Huigen brought jade Buddhas back from Myanmar —one seated, one reclining —and built this temple to house them.

> 🕖 Longhua Temple hours: 07:00-16:30
> 🚇 **Metro**: Lines 11/12, Longhua Station, Exit 1

## #10: Xintiandi

![Xintiandi](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/lujiazui_gw.webp)

A landmark that blends shikumen (stone-gate) architecture with modern commerce. The brick-and-tile exteriors of the old lane houses have been preserved, while the interiors have been given a contemporary makeover. After dark, themed bars offer live performances, and the international crowd makes Xintiandi feel like a European street.

> 🍺 Grab a beer on the outdoor terrace and soak up Shanghai's nightlife!
> 🏛Next door, the "Site of the First CPC National Congress" is open for free with ID

## #11: North Bund (🆕 New for 2026)

A fresh hotspot with fantastic photo angles! Bailian Plaza observation deck + North Bund Raffles City + Tilanqiao Station's rich history. Way fewer crowds than the Bund, with equally stunning night views.

## #12: Sinan Road

My personal favorite weekend strolling street. Along the way: Sun Yat-sen's former residence, Sinan Mansions, Zhou Enlai's former residence, St. Nicholas Church. I suggest entering from Middle Huaihai Road —grab a bite at Auntie Nian's noodle shop or a coffee at Antique Garden —then wander south.

> 🚶 Walk Sinan Road all the way south to Tianzifang —pair them together!
> 🚇 **Metro**: Line 13, Middle Huaihai Road Station, Exit 1

---

## #13: Shanghai Songcheng (Eternal Love Show)

![Shanghai Eternal Love Show](/images/shanghai/gowithmarkhazyl/shanghai_nianqing_show_gw.webp)

A massive show! Multimedia, lasers, lights, and smoke create stunning stage effects. The "Time Travel Street" recreates the Shanghai of the concession era and is a favorite photo spot for tourists. The whole venue is packed with content —you can easily spend a full day.

> 🎫 Tickets: from about 楼300
> 🚇 **Metro**: Expo Avenue Station

## #15: Shanghai Astronomy Museum (Dishui Lake)

🌍 **The world's largest planetarium!** Rich content, highly interactive, and the exhibitions set the standard for museums worldwide. The surrounding Dishui Lake area has many more attractions —you could easily spend 2-3 days exploring.

> 🚇 **Metro**: Line 16 to the end —Dishui Lake Station
